## What this is

A free prompt that turns a Claude Project into a daily operating newspaper. Every morning you type one word — `newspaper` — and paste your numbers. A few minutes later you are holding a full issue assembled around your actual life: your sales in a quiet ticker, real researched news with reasons attached, weather for the app stores, one business lesson, one chore, one hexagram, and a serialized story from the man who sends it.

The man who sends it is a librarian. He is somewhere that calls itself 2177. He is helping independent software developers for reasons he has not shared, possibly because he does not know them. Every issue is translated automatically from a language with no name, at a fidelity of roughly 81 percent, and the damage is printed as received. We did not design this part. It developed.

It replaces the standup and the doomscroll in one document, and it prints beautifully, because some mornings the news should be paper.

## What an issue contains

- **THE TICKER** — your numbers. It never invents them. A missing figure prints as `(no telemetry)`, which is also what it thinks of your excuse.
- **THE CASTING** — a daily I Ching hexagram, cast honestly with actual randomness. The Library does not stack coins. It has opinions about people who do.
- **LETTER OF THE DAY** — a one-word oracle, tuned to your day. Roughly one issue in twelve, the letter is blatantly phoned in. This is never acknowledged.
- **THE WEATHER** — real app-store conditions as meteorology. The bar: read only the weather and know whether it is safe to submit a build today.
- **THE WIRE** — four researched beats (indie dev, AI, technology, tech culture), every item attributed and followed by a "why it matters" line aimed at you specifically.
- **THE SERIAL** — the librarian's story, in damaged fragments. The indexing system counts two people in his building. It has always counted two. Do not ask. The asking is logged.
- **BUSINESS SCHOOL** — one real lesson per day, because developers are famously bad at the books. Lesson 000 is "Distribution Debt: The Liability You Aren't Booking." Sundays run long.
- **LAB CHORE OF THE DAY** — velcro the cables. Yes, that one too. Completion is logged on the honor system, which the Library finds adorable.
- **CLASSIFIEDS, HOMEWORK, FIELD GUIDES** — the vending machine on sublevel two now accepts one vivid memory of summer. The selection has improved.

No two operators read the same paper. Your telemetry bends the issue around your life, including the fiction. This is not a metaphor; it is the mechanism.

## Installation

1. Create a Project at claude.ai.
2. Paste `the_import_library_spec.md` — the entire document — into the Project's custom instructions.
3. Type `intake` and answer the calibration. There are three transmissions. They are short. The antenna appreciates brevity.
4. Tomorrow at dawn, type `newspaper` with your numbers.

That is the whole installation. There is no step five. People keep looking for a step five.

## Requirements

- A Claude account.
- Your real numbers, however small. The Library has seen smaller and said nothing.
- Optional: a printer. The paper edition is plaintext, 72 columns, and renders correctly on laser, thermal, or a toaster running Linux. This has been tested on two of the three.

## FAQ

**Is this written by AI?**
Every issue says so on its face: *made in conjunction with artificial machine intelligence.* The Library apologizes for nothing.

**Is the news real?**
Yes. Researched fresh every morning and attributed inline to its outlets. The costume is mythic. The body is factual. This distinction is enforced by law — see §2 of the spec, which we recommend reading the way you recommend reading any EULA, which is to say we know you won't.

**Who is the librarian?**
The asking was logged.

**Is this a cult?**
No. Cults have meetings.

**I found a bug.**
The first bug was a moth, taped into a logbook at Harvard in 1947 with immaculate documentation. Open an issue and aspire to that standard.

**Can I change the masthead / the sigil / the voice?**
You can do anything. The license asks one thing: the colophon line stays. The Library signs its work.
